About and Bio

This page lists some of my history and how I developed my interest in computer science. Also included are some of my professional experiences and educational accolades that influenced who I am today.

Early Life

I was born in Charlotte, NC in 1995. I have attended all levels of education here, and I consider Charlotte to be my home. One day I would like to travel and explore different parts of the world, but for now I am content where I am.


The best place to start my adventure in computer science would be with LSL, or Linden Scripting Language. This is a language that is inclusive to the game Second Life, which I played as a kid. It was my first ever exposure to computer programming, and I consider it to be the catalyst to my interest in programming as a whole. I never learned LSL, but I always thought it was fun to play around with!


Education

I attended James Martin Middle School here in Charlotte, NC. Although I did not start my coding adventures until high school, middle school is where I made the decision that my career path would follow computer science.


I attended Phillip O. Berry Academy of Technology in Charlotte, NC. P.O.B. was a technology magnet school, which I chose to attend for its computer science program. This is where I truly found my interest in computer programming (though not yet web development). At P.O.B, not all of the computer programming classes were required for the I.T. students, but I took them anyway, and earned top scores in each of the classes. These included: VB.Net, C# (XNA framework), and SAS Programming I and II. I graduated with a 4.84 weighted GPA, and earned some interesting accolades and awards while I attended. These included:

  • Certificate of Information Technology Studies, awarded for taking all IT classes available
  • SAS Certificate of Achievement, for traveling to Washington, D.C to present a SAS project at SAS Global Forum in 2014
  • STEM Awards for IT and STEM awards for Best of the Best, also for our SAS project
  • AP Scholar Award for taking and passing a number of Advanced Placement classes


I was accepted into the University of North Carolina at Charlotte for my first semester in Fall 2014 as a Computer Science major. I intended to follow my decision to pursue programming as a career, and the location of UNC Charlotte and well developed CS department led to my decision to attend. Currently I am still attending UNCC, and I plan to graduate in Spring 2018 with a Bachelor's in Computer Science and a concentration in Computing Systems.

My major does not follow Web Development, but rather software systems development. I decided in my sophomore year that web development was my personal interest, and that I would continue the path of my major and develop as a web developer as a personal goal. This way I would have multiple areas of expertise.
As it stands, these are my current accomplishments:

  • Chancellor's list: Fall 2014, Fall 2015
  • Dean's List: Spring 2015, Spring 2016
  • 3.74 GPA


Professional Experience

I would locally and remotely remove malware from my coworkers computers. I would also set up office spaces and computers as well as fix simple network issues and printer issues.


I also worked for UNC Charlotte my freshman spring semester as a Desktop Services Intern. Though this internship was short due to lack of work on my skill level, I did many of the same tasks that I worked on at Movement Mortgage.

  • Front-end modification such as creating an error page, adjusting styling, and importing information with Kendo UI.
  • Back-end creation, such as JavaScript functions, MVC controller and model interaction, and routing.
  • Database interaction, such as using C# to write methods to import data from a database and test it with MySql.
